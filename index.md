## 🎛️ Understanding The Features

The layout allows you to move sound parameters like a physical synthesizer. 

### Oscillators
The oscillator generates the raw sound wave. You can switch between different wave shapes to change the tone of your sound. The Fader section allows you to blend these signals.

### Filters
The filter blocks or allows certain frequencies. Use the Cutoff fader to strip away high frequencies for a darker, warmer sound. Use the Resonance fader to emphasize specific frequencies and add energy.

### LFO Section
The Low-Frequency Oscillator (LFO) automates changes to your sound over time. You can use this to create vibrato or rhythmic patterns that shift while you hold a note.

### Envelope
The envelope controls volume changes. The Attack fader determines how fast the sound starts. The Decay fader sets how fast the sound drops after it starts. The Sustain fader sets the level for the note length. The Release fader sets how long the sound fades after you let go of the key.
